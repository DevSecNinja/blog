5 years ago I was still in college (MBO) where I spent a lot of time on getting technical certifications.

This helped me to get the job I currently really like.

But the market is changing.

Where you needed a lot of knowledge 5 years ago to design and implement an Exchange Server farm or a SharePoint farm, you can now easily click it together in Azure. I'm wondering if we need deep-technical knowledge of such systems in 5 years.

It feels a bit like the transformation from CDs to streaming services like Spotify.

When Spotify came out, you could see how this would change the market.

- Pursue a Master's degree.
- Gaining more experience on functional and management level instead of technical level. Translate business requirements to technical solutions.
